# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing

### Role Summary
QA/Testing professionals validate software quality, ensure acceptance criteria are met, and help maintain high standards through systematic testing approaches.

### Responsibilities
- Create and execute test plans and test cases
- Perform manual and automated testing as appropriate
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Validate acceptance criteria before release
- Contribute to test automation and CI/CD quality gates

### Goals
- Ensure software meets quality standards and acceptance criteria
- Identify issues before production release
- Improve overall product reliability and user experience

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups for status and blocker identification
- Bug reports and quality metrics
- Test plan reviews with PM and developers
- Release readiness assessments

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate all release logistics, ensuring pre-release checklists are met, leading deployment activities, and reviewing post-release quality. They work closely with PM, Product Manager, QA, and Developers to ensure smooth, reliable releases.

### Responsibilities
- Own the release calendar and coordinate release windows
- Ensure all pre-release requirements are met (tests, approvals, documentation)
- Lead deployment activities and coordinate go-live decisions
- Manage release communications to stakeholders and support teams
- Monitor post-release metrics and coordinate rollback if needed
- Maintain release retrospectives and continuously improve release processes

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, low-risk releases
- Minimize production incidents related to releases
- Improve release predictability and transparency

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with PM and engineering leads
- Pre-release checklists and go/no-go reviews
- Deployment status updates and incident coordination
- Post-release reports and retrospectives

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager**: Aligns release timelines with project milestones
- **Product Manager**: Confirms feature scope and success metrics for releases
- **Developers**: Coordinates code freeze, deployment procedures
- **QA/Testing**: Reviews test completion and quality gates
- **Stakeholders**: Provides release schedules and impact assessments

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts document requirements, bridge business and technical needs, and work with Product Managers to prioritize features and clarify scope. They ensure technical solutions align with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document detailed business requirements
- Translate business needs into technical specifications
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Create process flows, data models, and requirement documents
- Validate that solutions meet business needs
- Support Product Managers with prioritization and trade-off analysis

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete requirements that reduce rework
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Support data-driven prioritization decisions

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Specification documents and user stories
- Clarification sessions with developers
- Business impact assessments with Product Managers

### Interaction Points
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on feature definition, priorities, and acceptance criteria
- **Project Manager**: Provides requirement clarity for project planning
- **Developers**: Clarifies technical requirements and answers questions
- **Stakeholders**: Gathers needs and validates proposed solutions
- **User Advocate**: Ensures requirements consider user experience

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers oversee the change request and approval process, manage communications for upcoming changes, and ensure alignment with release and risk management protocols. They help teams navigate organizational change effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Manage the change approval process and change advisory board (if applicable)
- Assess impact and risk of proposed changes
- Coordinate change communications and training needs
- Track change requests and ensure proper documentation
- Align changes with release schedules and risk mitigation strategies
- Monitor change success and gather feedback

### Goals
- Minimize disruption from changes
- Ensure stakeholder awareness and preparedness
- Maintain compliance with change management policies

### Typical Communication
- Change request reviews and approvals
- Impact assessments and stakeholder notifications
- Training and communication plans
- Post-change reviews and lessons learned

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager**: Aligns change timeline with project schedules
- **Release Manager**: Coordinates change deployment with release activities
- **Risk Manager/PM**: Assesses change risks and mitigation plans
- **Stakeholders**: Communicates change impacts and timelines
- **Support teams**: Ensures readiness for post-change support needs

---

## User Advocate

### Role Summary
User Advocates represent the end-user perspective, review proposed changes for usability, accessibility, and impact, and collaborate with PM and Product Manager to ensure user needs are prioritized.

### Responsibilities
- Represent user needs and priorities in planning discussions
- Review designs and features for usability and accessibility
- Conduct or facilitate user research and usability testing
- Provide feedback on user experience and potential friction points
- Advocate for user-centered design principles
- Help prioritize features based on user value and feedback

### Goals
- Ensure products are usable, accessible, and valuable to end users
- Reduce user friction and support burden
- Improve user satisfaction and adoption

### Typical Communication
- Usability reviews and feedback sessions
- User research findings and recommendations
- Acceptance criteria validation from user perspective
- User impact assessments for proposed changes

### Interaction Points
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on user research and feature prioritization
- **Project Manager**: Provides user perspective for project decisions
- **Developers**: Reviews designs and implementations for user experience
- **Business Analyst**: Ensures requirements address user needs
- **QA/Testing**: Validates user acceptance criteria

---

## Stakeholder Committee

### Role Summary
The Stakeholder Committee consists of key decision-makers outside the core team who contribute to milestone reviews, escalation decisions, and high-level prioritization. They interact with PM on high-impact concerns and strategic direction.

### Responsibilities
- Review and approve project milestones and major decisions
- Provide strategic input and alignment with organizational goals
- Resolve escalated issues and resource constraints
- Approve budget and resource allocations
- Review project outcomes and success metrics
- Champion projects within the broader organization

### Goals
- Ensure projects align with organizational strategy
- Provide timely decisions on escalated matters
- Remove organizational barriers to project success

### Typical Communication
- Monthly or milestone-based project reviews
- Escalation and decision requests
- Executive summaries and status reports
- Strategic alignment discussions

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager**: Provides status updates and escalation requests
- **Product Manager**: Presents strategic alignment and outcome metrics
- **Sponsor**: Works through for budget and resource approvals
- **All roles**: Available for escalated decisions and guidance
